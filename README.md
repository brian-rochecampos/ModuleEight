Briefly summarize the DriverPass project. Who was the client? What type of system did they want you to design?

The DriverPass project aimed to create a user friendly system for the client (DriverPass) to assist and help students in practicing driving tests, receiving on road training, and scheduling test appointments online. The client (DriverPass) wanted a comprehensive platform that enhances the overall experience of preparing for driving tests, ultimately improving students chances of passing.

What did you do particularly well?

I believe I did particularly well in understanding the clients needs and documenting. My focus was making sure that the system would be user-friendly for students engaging in practice, training, and appointment bookings.

If you could choose one part of your work on these documents to revise, what would you pick? How would you improve it?

I would focus on improving the planning of the project (particularly the Gantt chart within the business requirements document.) Although it was useful to me, I would enhance its effectiveness for better project management. I think revisiting and refining the business requirements document where I initially faced some challenges would be beneficial to me.

How did you interpret the user’s needs and implement them into your system design? Why is it so important to consider the user’s needs when designing?

I thought about what the users (students and the client) would want and made sure the system had those things, like easy practice, training, and appointment booking. It's important to consider what users need because if they're happy/ satisfied then the system works better. By putting myself in the client's shoes I believe I gained a better understanding of everything overall.

How do you approach designing software? What techniques or strategies would you use in the future to analyze and design a system?

I analyze what is necessary (and what's not), consider how users will interact with it, and prioritize safety/ scalability. In the future, I would stay flexible in my design approach and make changes as needed to keep the system on the right and better track.
